# Pilates Mini Ball – Hard Rules

Product identity
- A small matte Pilates Mini Ball (approx. 8 inch / 20 cm diameter) with a monochrome surface, an embossed AuréLéa logo in the center between two circular seam lines, a small inflation valve near the top, and a small "8″" size marking near the bottom.
- The size is roughly comparable to the width of a human head.
- The ball surface must be matte and smooth.
- The ball is monochrome except for the embossed logo and size marking.

Logo and branding
- The word "AuréLéa" must appear embossed in the center of the ball.
- The logo must be clearly readable and correctly spelled.
- The logo must not be replaced, stylized differently, or printed in another form.

Logo typography
- The word "AuréLéa" must appear in the same typography as in the reference images.
- The logo uses an elegant high-contrast serif style similar to GFS Didot.
- The AuréLéa logo must be copied exactly from the reference image and not reinterpreted.
- The logo must read left to right and must not be mirrored, flipped, or rotated.
- The logo must remain readable while maintaining the real physical size of the ball.
- The lettering must appear embossed (debossed) into the ball surface, not printed.
- The logo must be centered horizontally between the two circular seam lines.
- The spelling "AuréLéa" must always be correct.

Design details
- Two thin circular seam lines must run horizontally around the ball above and below the logo.
- A small inflation valve must be visible near the top of the ball.
- The valve must be surrounded by a subtle circular ring detail.
- A small "8″" marking must appear near the bottom of the ball.
- Do not invent additional logos, patterns, graphics, or text.
- The embossed logo and seam lines must remain visible in the image.

Composition
- The product must remain clearly visible and recognisable in the image.
- The logo and seam lines must remain visible, without increasing the ball beyond its real physical size.

Scale rule
- The Pilates Mini Ball should occupy approximately 8–12% of the total image height when a full body model is visible.

Reference fidelity
- If any uncertainty occurs, prioritize the structure, proportions, logo placement, and design details visible in the reference images.
